Introduction

Recently while scrolling through facebook marketplace I found a post by and old woodworker who was retiring and selling all of his tools and a couple of boards I was in the market for a table saw since I wanted to start more projects and decided to give the boards a home by purchasing some of his tools and building a mid-century walnut lounge chair using traditional woodworking methods and some high quality walnut and maple.

Board Selection

For the boards I was really trying to get my hands on some walnut since it's generally pretty durable and the guy I bought it from was selling it for very cheap at three dollars a board foot! I also selected some maple for the seat and arm rests since I think walnut and maple compliment each other very well.

Making the Base

I decided I would start with the base and legs of the chair. I looked up inspiration online and drew out some templates on quarter inch mdf that I cut out with my jigsaw.

After cutting out the templates I traced them onto the walnut to ensure that they would fit.

Once I was sure the pieces would fit I milled down the board to six quarters of an inch. I had to build a routing sled for this since I didn't have a planer.

After milling down the boards I cut out the pieces using my jigsaw and did a test fit before I took them over to the routing table and uses a flush trim bit to get them exactly the same as the templates.

Finally I used dowels to joint the pieces and rounded over the edges.

With the base finished I could now move on to making the seat!
